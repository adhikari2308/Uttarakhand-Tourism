Tungnath Temple

Tungnath Temple, located in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, is one of the highest Shiva temples in the world, perched at an altitude of 3,680 meters (12,073 feet) in the Garhwal Himalayas. This ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is considered the third of the five Panch Kedar temples. The temple is not only significant for its religious importance but also offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, making it a popular trekking destination for pilgrims and nature enthusiasts alike.

The trek to Tungnath begins from Chopta, a small village known as the 'Mini Switzerland of India.' The hike, which is about 3.5 kilometers, is relatively easy and can be completed in a few hours, making it accessible for people of all ages. Once at the temple, visitors can enjoy the serene atmosphere and partake in various rituals and prayers. Tungnath is also known for its stunning landscapes, lush greenery, and a variety of flora and fauna, making it a paradise for photographers and adventure seekers. The temple is open to pilgrims from April to November, as the region experiences heavy snowfall in winter.
