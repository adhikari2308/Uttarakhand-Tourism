Gangotri Temple

Gangotri Temple, situated in the Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand, India, is one of the four sacred shrines of the Char Dham pilgrimage. Dedicated to Goddess Ganga, the temple is perched at an elevation of 3,042 meters (9,977 feet) near the banks of the Bhagirathi River. This ancient temple, believed to have been built in the 18th century, is made of white granite and features a striking architectural style that attracts thousands of pilgrims and tourists each year.

The temple marks the origin of the Ganges River, making it a significant site for Hindu devotees. The Ganges is considered a sacred river in Hinduism, and it is believed that bathing in its waters cleanses sins and brings salvation. The journey to Gangotri is an adventure in itself, involving a scenic trek through the beautiful Himalayan landscape. The area around the temple is also famous for its stunning natural beauty, lush green forests, and the majestic peaks of the Himalayas.
