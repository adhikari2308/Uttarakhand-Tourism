Abott Mount

Abott Mount is a picturesque hill station located in the Champawat district of Uttarakhand, India. Situated at an altitude of 6,400 feet, this serene location offers panoramic views of the Himalayan peaks and is surrounded by dense pine and oak forests. The area was established in the early 20th century by a British businessman, John Harold Abott, and retains an old-world charm with its colonial-era cottages and lush landscapes.

Known for its tranquil environment and breathtaking sunrise and sunset views, Abott Mount is a paradise for nature lovers, trekkers, and solitude seekers. The region also boasts rich cultural heritage with temples and quaint village settings, making it an ideal destination for those seeking spiritual and cultural insights. Visitors can explore various attractions, including the Mukteshwar Temple, the Mayawati Ashram, and the Abott Mount Church.

Abott Mount is also popular among bird watchers and wildlife enthusiasts, as the surrounding forests are home to diverse flora and fauna. With its peaceful atmosphere and abundant natural beauty, Abott Mount remains one of the lesser-explored gems of Uttarakhand, offering a refreshing retreat for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.
